General information about Ouarzazate

Ouarzazate, a city with a population of around 80,000, is the capital of its namesake province and sits at an altitude of 1,160 meters between the High Atlas mountain ranges. Founded in 1928 as a small Berber settlement, it has grown into an important center for handicrafts and trade due to its prime location. It serves as a major transportation hub in southern Morocco and a popular tourist destination. A trip to Ouarzazate is a must for any vacation in Morocco. The 200 km journey from Marrakech takes around 4 hours, but it is recommended to allow more time due to the scenic route and sights along the way.

There are a few interesting attractions to discover in the city itself, with almost everything revolving around films and film productions. However, also close to Ouarzazate are real highlights to discover, such as the world-famous UNESCO World Heritage Site Ait-Ben-Haddou. Here are the best things to do when you visit Ouarzazate:


Ksar of Ait-Ben-Haddou

Steeped in history and cinematic charm, Ksar Ait Ben Haddou is a fortified village near Ouarzazate that beckons explorers. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a collection of traditional earthen buildings, known as kasbahs, that rise dramatically from the desert landscape. Wander its labyrinthine alleys, imagining life in bygone eras, and delve into the rich Berber heritage that thrives within its walls. Ksar Ait Ben Haddou has also served as a backdrop for numerous Hollywood films, making it a recognizable landmark for movie buffs as well. Don't miss your chance to experience this unique and captivating location


The Kasbah of Taourirt, the pearl of Ouarzazate

The most important attraction right in the city of Ouarzazate is the impressive Kasbah Taourirt, a residential castle made of rammed earth, which was built in the early 20th century. This Kasbah is still very well-preserved and a third of it has been opened to the public for visits. Guided tours can be booked on site.

Souks of Ouarzazate

Immerse yourself in the sights, smells, and sounds of Ouarzazate's vibrant souks. Haggle for treasures like Berber crafts and textiles, savor mint tea in bustling cafes, and mingle with friendly locals.
